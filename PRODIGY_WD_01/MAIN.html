About Blackpink

Blackpink is a South Korean girl group formed by YG Entertainment, consisting of members Jisoo, Jennie, Rosé, and Lisa. The group debuted on August 8, 2016, with their single album "Square One," which spawned "Whistle," their first number-one song in South Korea, as well as "Boombayah," their first number-one hit on the Billboard World Digital Songs chart.

Blackpink is known for their diverse discography, which includes various genres like K-pop, EDM, hip hop, and trap music. They have set numerous records throughout their career, including being the highest-charting female Korean act on the Billboard Hot 100, peaking at number 13 with "Ice Cream" (2020), and on the Billboard 200, peaking at number 2 with "The Album" (2020), which is also the first-ever album by a Korean girl group to sell more than one million copies.

Their music videos are some of the most viewed online, with hits like "Ddu-Du Ddu-Du," "Kill This Love," and "How You Like That" each garnering over a billion views on YouTube. Blackpink has also been recognized with numerous awards and nominations, including multiple MTV Video Music Awards and a Teen Choice Award. They are also the first K-pop girl group to perform at Coachella, one of the biggest music festivals in the United States.
